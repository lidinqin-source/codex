# Operating Review

## Purpose

Use the operating review to answer four business questions:

1. Are we on track against the affiliate goal?
2. Which country, publisher, source, or platform issue explains the movement?
3. What is reliable evidence versus an attribution or data-quality caveat?
4. What should the operator do next week?

## Core Scenarios

- Weekly business review: summarize target pacing, contribution, movements, caveats, and next actions.
- Target miss diagnosis: isolate country, publisher, traffic, conversion, platform, and tracking causes.
- Market expansion or country drilldown: compare market baseline, affiliate share, and partner mix by country.
- Publisher operations: identify growth partners, zero-revenue traffic, unmapped IDs, migration targets, and follow-up owners.
- Platform reconciliation: explain matched, GA4-only, platform-only, out-of-market, and missing-ID order groups.
- Commission or payout risk: separate sales performance from platform commission liability and validation status.
- Awin residual migration: preserve residual traffic evidence while treating it as migration work, not active Europe platform performance.

## Recommended Structure

For production weekly reports, use:

1. `Overview by Region`
2. `Overview by Country & Goal Health`
3. `Drill-down by Country`
4. `New Week Action Plan`
5. `Appendix / Audit Evidence`

Use `references/weekly-report-standard.md` for the full management-facing layout when producing a weekly report artifact.

## Operating Language

- Say `behind`, `on track`, `ahead`, `data incomplete`, or `evidence conflict`.
- Tie every diagnosis to an operating lever: publisher activation, tracking repair, platform refresh, payout review, target configuration, or market prioritization.
- Keep leadership sections concise. Put raw evidence, long tables, and row-level exceptions in appendix artifacts.

## Action Plan Standard

Each action should include:

- owner or responsible function
- action
- evidence or trigger
- expected impact
- deadline or next check
- dependency or decision request when blocked

Do not create vague actions like "monitor performance" unless the next check, threshold, and owner are explicit.
